## Activity 1: GYMNASTICS / TUMBLING PROGRAM

### üìù Program Description
Structured movement program teaching basic gymnastics skills in a safe, supportive environment. Focus on body awareness, balance, coordination, and controlled risk-taking.

### üéØ What Reyansh Will Learn
- **Physical Skills:** Forward rolls, balance beam walking, jumping, hanging
- **Risk Management:** Assessing safe vs. unsafe challenges
- **Resilience:** Getting up after falls, trying again
- **Body Awareness:** Understanding capabilities and limitations

### üèÜ IB Learner Profile Development
- **Risk-taker (PRIMARY):** Trying new moves, overcoming fear
- **Balanced:** Physical fitness and body control
- **Reflective:** "What worked? What can I try differently?"
- **Principled:** Following safety rules, taking turns

### üë• Typical Class Structure (45-60 minutes)
1. **Warm-up Circle** (5-10 min): Songs, stretches, movement games
2. **Skill Stations** (25-35 min): Rotation through 4-6 stations
   - Balance beam (low to ground)
   - Forward rolls on mat
   - Hanging/swinging on bars
   - Jumping/landing practice
   - Obstacle course
3. **Free Play** (5-10 min): Explore equipment with supervision
4. **Cool-down** (5 min): Stretching, stamps/stickers, goodbye song

### üìã What to Look for in a Program
- **Safety First:**
  - Thick mats under all equipment
  - Equipment scaled for toddlers (low bars, low beams)
  - Instructors actively spotting
  - 1:6 ratio or better
- **Appropriate Progression:**
  - Skills taught in small steps
  - No pressure to perform
  - Alternative easier versions offered
- **Positive Environment:**
  - Praise for trying, not just succeeding
  - "You did it!" vs. "You're the best!"
  - No comparison between children

### üí∞ Typical Investment
- **Cost:** $60-150/month (1-2x/week)
- **Commitment:** 8-12 week sessions
- **Equipment:** Comfortable athletic clothing, bare feet or gymnastics shoes

### üè† At-Home Extensions
- **Living Room Obstacle Course:**
  - Cushions to jump on/over
  - Tape lines on floor for balance walking
  - Furniture tunnel crawls (safe spaces only)
- **Mirror Play:**
  - Practice moves in front of mirror
  - Build body awareness
- **"Gymnastics Show":**
  - Perform for family
  - Builds confidence and reflection

### ‚ö†Ô∏è Red Flags
- ‚ùå High bars or equipment children could fall from
- ‚ùå Pressure to perform specific skills
- ‚ùå Unattended children on equipment
- ‚ùå No safety briefing for parents/kids

### ‚úÖ Green Flags
- ‚úÖ Multiple instructors/spotters
- ‚úÖ Classes divided by age (3-5 separate from 6-8)
- ‚úÖ Parent observation area
- ‚úÖ Progressive skill charts showing growth

- **Risk-taker:** Trying new sports, learning from misses
- **Balanced:** Physical activity, varied movement
- **Communicator:** Calling for passes, expressing needs

### üë• Typical Class Structure (45-60 minutes)

  - No yelling or pressure
- **Age-Appropriate Equipment:**
  - Smaller, softer balls
  - Lower goals/nets
  - Shorter playing area
- **Skill Development:**
  - Skills broken into small steps
  - Individual practice before group play
  - Modifications for different abilities

### üí∞ Typical Investment
- **Cost:** $50-120/month (1-2x/week)
- **Commitment:** 6-12 week sessions
- **Equipment:** Athletic shoes, water bottle, comfortable clothes

### üè† At-Home Extensions
- **Backyard Practice:**
  - Kick ball back and forth
  - Throw and catch
  - Set up "goals" with cones
- **Family Sports Time:**
  - Weekly family soccer/basketball game
  - Make it silly and fun, not competitive
- **Watch Sports Together:**
  - Age-appropriate sports content
  - Talk about teamwork and sportsmanship

### ‚ö†Ô∏è Red Flags
- ‚ùå Keeping score or announcing winners
- ‚ùå Children sitting out as "punishment"
- ‚ùå Focus on "star players"
- ‚ùå Parents coaching from sidelines aggressively

### ‚úÖ Green Flags
- ‚úÖ All children participate equally
- ‚úÖ Rotating positions/roles
- ‚úÖ Celebration of all efforts
- ‚úÖ Modifications for different skill levels

## Activity 4: ROCK CLIMBING (Indoor, for Kids)

### üìù Program Description
Introduction to indoor rock climbing with child-sized walls, auto-belay systems, and bouldering areas designed for young children.

### üéØ What Reyansh Will Learn
- **Physical Skills:** Climbing technique, grip strength, problem-solving routes
- **Risk Assessment:** Evaluating holds, choosing paths
- **Persistence:** Trying difficult routes multiple times
- **Trust:** Trusting equipment and instructors

### üèÜ IB Learner Profile Development
- **Risk-taker (PRIMARY):** Climbing higher, trying new routes
- **Thinker:** Planning climbing path, problem-solving
- **Reflective:** "What worked? What didn't? Try again differently"
- **Balanced:** Physical challenge, mental focus

### üë• Typical Class Structure (60 minutes)
1. **Safety Briefing** (10 min): Equipment check, rules, warm-up stretches
2. **Bouldering Practice** (20 min): Low walls, no harness, thick mats
3. **Auto-Belay Climbing** (20 min): Higher walls with automatic safety system
4. **Games & Challenges** (10 min): "Reach the red hold," "climb like spider"
5. **Cool-down & Reflection** (10 min): Stretching, share favorite moment

### üìã What to Look for in a Program
- **Age-Appropriate Design:**
  - Walls not too high for beginners (8-12 feet for bouldering)
  - Colorful, fun hold designs (animals, shapes)
  - Clear route marking by difficulty
- **Excellent Safety:**
  - Auto-belay systems (no belayer needed)
  - Very thick landing mats
  - Staff supervision at all times
  - Proper fitting harnesses
- **Encouraging Environment:**
  - No pressure to climb higher
  - Celebrate all attempts
  - Teach "safe falling" technique

### üí∞ Typical Investment
- **Cost:** $70-150/month (1x/week) or day passes $15-25
- **Commitment:** Drop-in friendly or 4-8 week courses
- **Equipment:** Rental shoes usually included; comfortable athletic wear

### üè† At-Home Extensions
- **Playground Climbing:**
  - Practice at local playgrounds
  - Talk about choosing safe vs. unsafe holds
- **"The Floor is Lava":**
  - Climb on safe furniture (with supervision)
  - Builds problem-solving and risk assessment
- **Growth Mindset Talks:**
  - "What route do you want to try next time?"
  - "What was hard today? What got easier?"

### ‚ö†Ô∏è Red Flags
- ‚ùå Insufficient safety equipment
- ‚ùå Walls too high for age group (15+ feet for beginners)
- ‚ùå Inadequate supervision
- ‚ùå Peer pressure to climb higher

### ‚úÖ Green Flags
- ‚úÖ Dedicated kids' area
- ‚úÖ Staff trained in youth programming
- ‚úÖ Progressive skill levels
- ‚úÖ Parent observation areas

## Activity 8: SCIENCE EXPLORATION CLUB

### üìù Program Description
Hands-on science experiments and discovery activities designed for young children. Focus on observation, prediction, and exploration rather than formal science concepts.

### üéØ What Reyansh Will Learn
- **Scientific Process:** Observe, predict, experiment, conclude
- **Topics:** Magnets, water, light, plants, animals, weather, simple machines
- **Skills:** Measuring, comparing, recording observations
- **Curiosity:** Asking "why" and "what if" questions

### üèÜ IB Learner Profile Development
- **Inquirer (PRIMARY):** Asking questions, seeking answers
- **Knowledgeable:** Learning across scientific disciplines
- **Thinker:** Problem-solving, critical thinking
- **Risk-taker:** Testing hypotheses, learning from failed experiments
- **Reflective:** "What happened? Why? What would happen if...?"

### üë• Typical Class Structure (60 minutes)
1. **Wonder Circle** (10 min):
   - "What do you wonder about...?"
   - Introduction to day's topic
   - Make predictions
2. **Experiment Station Rotations** (35 min):
   - 3-4 hands-on experiments
   - Small groups rotate
   - Guided by questions, not instructions
3. **Discovery Time** (10 min): Free exploration with materials
4. **Reflection Circle** (5 min):
   - "What did you discover?"
   - "What surprised you?"
   - "What do you want to try next?"

**Example Monthly Themes:**
- **Month 1: Water Science**
  - Sink or float experiments
  - Ice melting observations
  - Water and oil mixing
  - Absorbing materials test

- **Month 2: Forces & Motion**
  - Ramps and rolling (speed, distance)
  - Magnet exploration
  - Pushing and pulling
  - Balance and weight

- **Month 3: Living Things**
  - Plant growth observations
  - Insect watching
  - Animal habitat exploration
  - Life cycle activities

- **Month 4: Light & Color**
  - Shadows and light
  - Color mixing
  - Prisms and rainbows
  - Reflections

### üìã What to Look for in a Program
- **Inquiry-Based Approach:**
  - Children asking questions (not just answering)
  - Open-ended explorations
  - "What do YOU think?" emphasized
  - Multiple right answers/approaches
- **Safety:**
  - Age-appropriate materials only
  - Non-toxic substances
  - Supervision for all experiments
  - Clear safety rules
- **Developmentally Appropriate:**
  - Hands-on, not worksheets
  - Observation-based, not memorization
  - Short experiments (10-15 min each)
  - Visual, tactile learning

### üí∞ Typical Investment
- **Cost:** $60-120/month (1x/week or bi-weekly)
- **Commitment:** 4-8 week sessions by theme
- **Equipment:** All materials provided; lab coat sometimes included

### üè† At-Home Extensions
- **Kitchen Science:**
  - Baking (measuring, mixing, chemical reactions)
  - Ice experiments
  - Sink/float with bath toys
  - Dissolving (sugar vs. sand in water)

- **Backyard Science:**
  - Nature observations
  - Bug watching with magnifying glass
  - Weather tracking
  - Shadow observations at different times

- **Science Supplies Box:**
  - Magnifying glass
  - Magnets
  - Measuring cups/spoons
  - Collection containers
  - Observation journal

### ‚ö†Ô∏è Red Flags
- ‚ùå Dangerous materials for age group
- ‚ùå Teacher doing experiments (not children)
- ‚ùå Emphasis on "right answers"
- ‚ùå Worksheets and memorization

### ‚úÖ Green Flags
- ‚úÖ Children leading experiments
- ‚úÖ "Messy play" is welcomed
- ‚úÖ Questions are celebrated
- ‚úÖ Failed experiments are learning opportunities

**Why Perfect for Reyansh:**

## Activity 9: NATURE & OUTDOOR EXPLORATION

### üìù Program Description
Regular outdoor sessions in natural settings (parks, forests, nature preserves). Focus on environmental education, observation, and hands-on exploration.

### üéØ What Reyansh Will Learn
- **Nature Knowledge:** Plants, animals, ecosystems, weather, seasons
- **Observation Skills:** Noticing details, tracking changes
- **Environmental Stewardship:** Caring for nature, conservation basics
- **Outdoor Skills:** Safe exploration, using tools (magnifying glass, etc.)

### üèÜ IB Learner Profile Development
- **Inquirer (PRIMARY):** Investigating natural world
- **Knowledgeable:** Environmental science, ecology
- **Caring:** Respect for nature, conservation
- **Risk-taker:** Outdoor challenges (climbing, balancing)
- **Reflective:** Seasonal changes, cause and effect in nature
- **Open-minded:** Diverse ecosystems and living things

### üë• Typical Class Structure (90-120 minutes)
1. **Gathering Circle** (10 min):
   - Safety reminders
   - Day's focus/question
   - Weather/season discussion

2. **Guided Nature Walk** (30 min):
   - Observe specific things (birds, plants, insects)
   - Collection time (leaves, rocks, sticks - take-only what's abundant)
   - Nature journaling stops

3. **Free Exploration** (30 min):
   - Supervised but child-led
   - Climbing, balancing on logs
   - Digging, building
   - Water play (if available)

4. **Focused Activity** (20 min):
   - Nature craft (leaf rubbings, stick structures)
   - Planting or gardening
   - Animal tracking
   - Weather experiments

5. **Closing Circle** (10 min):
   - Share discoveries
   - Discuss observations
   - Plan next session

**Seasonal Variations:**
- **Spring:** Plant growth, baby animals, bird watching
- **Summer:** Insects, water ecology, tree identification
- **Fall:** Leaf changes, seed dispersal, migration
- **Winter:** Animal tracks, dormancy, weather patterns

### üìã What to Look for in a Program
- **Qualified Naturalist:**
